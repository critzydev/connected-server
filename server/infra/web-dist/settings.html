Settings

Relay

Where your phone sends the stream. This is your VPS’s WHIP ingest endpoint — the relay then keeps the broadcast alive on Twitch.

Twitch

Easiest: skip this entirely — link your Twitch account under Channel rewards & sounds below and the relay fetches (and auto-rotates) the stream key itself. This manual field is the no-link alternative: your key from Twitch › Settings › Stream, set as TWITCH_STREAM_KEY on the relay.

Keep this private — anyone with it can stream to your channel.

Chat & voice

Your Twitch channel, so the cockpit and any companion phone can read chat. Reading is anonymous — no login. To hear chat aloud without it entering your broadcast, open /monitor on a second phone and turn on TTS there.

Studio

Link your phones into one rig: the camera phone streams, a companion phone runs the /monitor studio — chat, alerts, and TTS with shared mute/skip. Enter the same session code on every device (it’s the SESSION_CODE from your relay’s deploy.env).

Invite people to your rig — chat, alerts, program view, each permission your choice — from the Crew page. Only you can ever go live.

Only set this if your hub runs somewhere other than your relay (e.g. ws://localhost:8787/ws in development).

Output

Defaults are tuned for cellular. Lower the bitrate if your uplink is weak — the relay keeps you covered either way.
